Our Mission

What We
Stand For

The name Timion comes from the Greek — meaning "valuable, held in honour." It reflects everything we do: seeing the worth in every child, regardless of disability.

Founded 19 years ago in South Africa by Daniel and Anika Meyer, Timion NPC supports children with cerebral palsy and other disabilities in the townships of the Eastern Cape — through assistive devices, family empowerment, therapist training, and a growing network of care.

Timion as an
Organisation

The 2025/26 reporting period saw Timion invest deliberately in stronger internal structures — not to introduce new systems, but to simplify, stabilise and better integrate existing processes into daily life.

A key milestone was the first Management Planning Retreat in January 2026. Two days of honest reflection, realistic prioritisation, and a decisive shift from annual to quarterly planning — a change that proved both relieving and clarifying.

Chapter 02

The Workshop &
Production

Timion's workshop produces functional assistive devices for children with cerebral palsy. Craftsmanship, training, and precision are the foundation of everything made here.

New Machinery

  • Multi-Spindle Drill Press — reduces production time and improves precision
  • Belt Sander — calibrated by a Swiss engineer; markedly better surface quality
  • Industrial Compressor — supports new machines and improves spray-painting finish

Carpentry Training

Individual hands-on coaching during live production proved far more effective than group lessons. At least two Swiss civilian volunteers with trade qualifications worked in the workshop simultaneously — enabling training and productive output at the same time.

Chapter 04

Timion Village

Most construction work was completed through the team's own hands-on effort — saving costs and deepening ownership of the site.

Volunteer Bungalow Renovation

The former caretaker's bungalow was converted for volunteers and civilian service workers. An additional bedroom added between two bungalows allows up to 7 mothers and their children with CP to be hosted simultaneously.

In-Reach at Timion Village

Families spend two days and one night at Timion Village. All assistive devices are brought for checking and adjustment. The calm environment creates space for meaningful conversation and support — safely, without sending staff into high-risk areas.

Timion Village

The Vegetable
Garden

Running since the COVID-19 lockdown, the garden has become a permanent fixture of Timion Village life — providing fresh produce for staff, camps, Alena's Love, and the wider community.

  • Fresh vegetables sold weekly to staff at fair prices
  • Regular sales to neighbours and friends
  • Supplies to soup kitchens and children's homes
  • New online platform: veggies.timion.org
  • Mothers in a local township supported in starting their own gardens
Next Step
The garden is not yet self-sustaining. The vision is a more independent project that operates with less direct leadership input — freeing Daniel for broader organisational responsibilities while growing the garden's social impact.
Chapter 08

Training External
Therapists

Awareness of assistive devices for children with cerebral palsy remains low across South Africa. By training therapists in hospitals and medical centres, Timion extends its reach far beyond what direct service alone could achieve.

Government-funded device prescriptions create real revenue — making therapist training a direct driver of Timion's financial sustainability. Families who cannot access public funding receive devices as donations.

Special Highlight
A multi-week intensive training was delivered with staff and parents of Rainbow Angels Kita in Humansdorp — combining device knowledge with hands-on family empowerment.
